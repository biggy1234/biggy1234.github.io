Turkish Desserts

Bio on the Turkish background

Turkish desserts have a very strong impact on thier culture. The desserts fulfill an important social and ritual function. One of the desserts named Halvah (Helva) is offered on the occasion of major changes in people's lives like a birth or a death.

The Dessert I Recomend

Lokum

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups of water
  • 3 cups of granulated sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of light corn syrup
  • 1/2 cup of orange juice
  • 3 tablespoons of orange zest
  • 3 envelopes of unflavored gelatin
  • 3/4 cup of cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup of cold water
  • 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup of chopped pistachio nuts

Directions

  1. Bring 1 1/2 cups of water, sugar, and corn syrup to a boil over medium-high heat in a large saucepan. Cook, stirring frequently, until the temperature reaches 240 degrees F (115 degrees C) on a candy themometer. Set aside and keep hot.
  2. Stir together orange juice and orange zest, sprinkle with gelatin, and set aside. In a small bowl, dissolve cornstarch in 1/2 cup of cold water, then stir into hot syrup. Place over medium-low heat, and simmer, stirring gently, until very thick.
  3. Remove syrup from heat, stir orange juice mixture, vanilla, and pistacios. Sprinkle a 8x8-inch pan generoulsy with confectioners' sugar. Pour the Turkish delight (Lokum) into the pan, and let cool in a cool, dry place (not the refrigerator) until set, 3 to 4 hours.
  4. When cool, sprinkle the top with another thick layer of powdered sugar. Cut into 1-inch squares, and dredge each well with confectioners' sugar. Store at room temperature in an airtight container.

Lokma

Ingrediants

    For the dough

  • 1 cup of lukewarm water
  • 2 cups of all purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of instant dry yeast
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 cup of sunflower oil, for frying
  • 1/4 cup of extra oil, to dip a teaspoon when dropping dough balls into hot oil

    • Syrup

  • 2 cups of sugar
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice

Directions

  1. Start with making the syrup.
  2. Mix sugar and water in a pot and bring it to boil.
  3. Let it boil for about 15 minutes until it gets a little sticky.
  4. Add lemon juice and boil for a further 5 minutes.
  5. Let it cold.
  6. Mix lukewarm water, sugar and yeast in a cup.
  7. Sift flour in a large bowl.
  8. Pour yeast mixture in it and mix it with an electric mixer.
  9. Add egg and salt and mix. You will get a soft and sticky batter, a little thicker than a regular cake batter.
  10. Cover the bowl and wait about an hour until it doubles in size.
  11. Heat oil in a deep pot until boiling point.
  12. Take some dough in your hand and squeeze it so that you see a small amount between your thumb and forefinger.
  13. Dip a teaspoon into a little oil and use it to drop that rounded bit of dough into boiling oil.
  14. Fry it until golden for about a minute.
  15. Repeat the same steps until you finish the dough.
  16. Transfer fried dough balls immediately into cold syrup and leave them in it as long as you want. The longer the sweeter.
  17. Take them from syrup when they absorb enough of it and serve warm.
